# The Debian Family Tree (Non-Ubuntu)

This list tracks Linux distributions based directly on **Debian** that maintain their own identity and tools while staying close to the "Universal Operating System."

> **Disclaimer:** This list is for educational and tracking purposes only. Inclusion in this list does not constitute an endorsement of a project's privacy practices or security auditing.

> **Note:** This list excludes Ubuntu-based distributions to focus on the direct Debian ecosystem.

## Daily Drivers (Ease of Use)
* **[LMDE](https://www.linuxmint.com/download_lmde.php)** (Linux Mint Debian Edition) - The Mint experience without the Ubuntu base.
* **[MX Linux](https://mxlinux.org/)** - Features the custom MXTools suite for system management.
* **[Peppermint OS](https://peppermintos.com/)** - Lightweight and cloud-integrated.
* **[Neptune](https://neptuneos.com/)** - Polished KDE Plasma experience on a stable Debian base.
* **[SpiralLinux](https://spirallinux.github.io/)** - Pure Debian with pre-configured Btrfs and ZRAM.
* **[SolydXK](https://solydxk.com/)** - Stability-focused with XFCE or KDE desktops.
* **[Lingmo OS](https://lingmo.org/)** / **[Mauna Linux](https://maunalinux.org/)** / **[Br OS](https://br-os.com/)** - Modern distributions with beautiful custom aesthetics.
* **[Synex](https://synex.ar/)** - Minimalist, fast-booting Argentine distro that ships without pre-installed bloat.
* **[Maple Linux](https://maplelinux.ca/)** - A Canadian, privacy focused desktop aimed at digital sovereignty.

## Modern & Atomic (Immutable)
* **[Vanilla OS](https://vanillaos.org/)** - Atomic updates; utilizes `apx` to run apps from other ecosystems.
* **[blendOS](https://blendos.co/)** - Combines multiple distributions into a single atomic system.
* **[Nitrux](https://nxos.org/)** - Immutable, systemd-free (OpenRC), and relies heavily on AppImages.
* **[IncusOS](https://linuxcontainers.org/incus-os/)** - A minimal, immutable appliance OS purpose-built for running Incus containers.

## High Performance & Gaming
* **[Siduction](https://siduction.org/)** - A rolling release based on Debian Sid (unstable).
* **[SparkyLinux](https://sparkylinux.org/)** - Includes a "Gameover" edition pre-loaded with gaming tools and drivers.
* **[PikaOS](https://pika-os.com/)** - Focused on gaming with high-performance kernel patches.

## Multimedia & Creative
* **[AV Linux](http://www.bandshed.net/avlinux/)** (MX Edition) - Preconfigured with a low-latency kernel for pro-audio and video work.

## Minimalist
* **[BashCore](https://bashcore.org/)** - Ultra-minimal, branding-free CLI environment.
* **[Slax](https://www.slax.org/)** - A modular, "pocket" OS that runs entirely from a USB stick.
* **[dCore](http://tinycorelinux.net/dcore/)** - A Tiny Core variant that imports Debian packages directly into its unique RAM-based architecture.

## Performance & Old Hardware (Lightweight)
* **[antiX](https://antixlinux.com/)** - No systemd; designed to run on extremely old hardware.
* **[CrunchBang++](https://crunchbangplusplus.org/)** (#!++) - A continuation of the minimalist Openbox legend.
* **[BunsenLabs](https://www.bunsenlabs.org/)** - The spiritual successor to CrunchBang, featuring a highly-tuned Openbox setup.
* **[Window Maker Live](http://wmlive.sourceforge.net/)** - Provides a retro 90s NeXTSTEP aesthetic.
* **[Puppy Linux (DPup)](https://puppylinux-woof-ce.github.io/)** - Runs entirely in RAM for lightning speed.
* **[Elive](https://www.elivecd.org/)** - Uses the Enlightenment desktop to bring high-end looks to old PCs.
* **[Damn Small Linux](http://www.damnsmalllinux.org/)** - Reborn to stay under 700MB for CD compatibility.
* **[wattOS](https://www.planetwatt.com/)** - Focused on low power consumption and simplicity.
* **[Q4OS](https://q4os.org/)** - Features the Trinity Desktop; can mimic Windows XP/7.
* **[Commodore OS Vision](https://www.commodoreos.net/)** - A tribute to the retro Commodore aesthetic.

## Security, Privacy, and Stealth
* **[Qubes OS](https://www.qubes-os.org/)** - Uses Xen VMs to isolate tasks for high security.
* **[Tails](https://tails.net/)** - The amnesic live OS used for total anonymity.
* **[Whonix](https://www.whonix.org/)** - Routes everything through Tor via a dual-VM gateway setup.
* **[Kodachi](https://www.digi77.com/linux-kodachi/)** - A hardened live OS focused on anti-forensics.
* **[Septor](https://septor.sourceforge.io/)** - A Debian-based tool for anonymous web surfing.
* **[Kicksecure](https://www.kicksecure.com/)** - A security-hardened Debian base that can be run standalone or as a VM host.

## IT and SysAdmin Toolbox
* **[Kali Linux](https://www.kali.org/)** - The industry standard for penetration testing.
* **[Parrot OS](https://www.parrotsec.org/)** - A security-focused distro designed for dev and pentesting.
* **[GParted Live](https://gparted.org/livecd.php)** / **[Dr. Parted](https://dr-parted-live.sourceforge.io/)** - Dedicated tools for disk management and partitioning.
* **[Grml](https://grml.org/)** / **[Finnix](https://www.finnix.org/)** / **[Knoppix](http://www.knopper.net/knoppix/index-en.html)** - The "Emergency Kits" for repairing broken systems.
* **[SysLinuxOS](https://syslinuxos.com/)** / **[Kaisen](https://kaisenlinux.org/)** - All-in-one systems for network and system administration.

## Servers, Cloud, and Self-Hosting
* **[Proxmox VE](https://www.proxmox.com/en/proxmox-ve)** - An enterprise-grade virtualization platform.
* **[TrueNAS SCALE](https://www.truenas.com/truenas-scale/)** - The open-source standard for high-performance NAS and ZFS.
* **[OpenMediaVault](https://www.openmediavault.org/)** - A user-friendly NAS solution for home hardware.
* **[VitalPBX](https://vitalpbx.com/)** - Turns a standard Debian install into a full-featured phone system.
* **[umbrelOS](https://umbrel.com/)** / **[StartOS](https://start9.com/)** / **[YunoHost](https://yunohost.org/)** - Simplified "one-click" self-hosting for personal clouds.
* **[Univention (UCS)](https://www.univention.com/products/ucs/)** - Professional Debian for corporate identity and infrastructure management.

## Network & Radio Engineering
* **[Live Raizo](https://sourceforge.net/projects/live-raizo/)** - A lab environment for simulating network topologies.
* **[Skywave Linux](https://skywavelinux.com/)** - Preconfigured for Software Defined Radio (SDR) enthusiasts.
* **[VyOS](https://vyos.io/)** - An enterprise-grade, CLI-driven network operating system for routers.

## Embedded, IoT, and Media
* **[Raspberry Pi OS](https://www.raspberrypi.com/software/operating-systems/)** - The official OS for the Raspberry Pi hardware family.
* **[DietPi](https://dietpi.com/)** - Extremely optimized and lightweight base for SBCs.
* **[OSMC](https://osmc.tv)** - A dedicated, minimal Kodi media center.
* **[LinuxCNC](http://linuxcnc.org/)** - A specialized distro for controlling heavy machinery and 3D printers.

## Mobile Distros
* **[Mobian](https://mobian-project.org/)** - Bringing the "Universal Operating System" to mobile phones.
* **[PureOS](https://pureos.net/)** - FSF-endorsed; powers the Librem 5 for full convergence.
* **[Droidian](https://droidian.org/)** - Debian-based layer for running on top of Android kernels.
* **[LuneOS](https://www.webos-ports.org/wiki/Main_Page)** - The community successor to Palm's webOS.
* **[FuriOS](https://furilabs.com/)** - Built for the FuriLabs FLX1; integrates Waydroid for app support.

## Philosophy and Geopolitics
> **Warning:** Several distributions in this section are state-sponsored or have faced allegations of data collection/telemetry. Research thoroughly before use.

* **[Devuan](https://www.devuan.org/)** - The Debian fork for those wanting init-freedom and no systemd.
* **[Deepin](https://www.deepin.org/)** - Known for its aesthetic DDE, but Software Center embeds telemetry sending usage data to Chinese analytics service by default. Can be disabled via settings, but opaque origin raises significant privacy concerns. Research thoroughly before use.
* **[GuideOS](https://guideos.de/)** - A German community project and experiment from the Linux Guides forum. Language is limited to German for now.
* **[Endless OS](https://endlessos.com/)** / **[Emmabuntüs](https://emmabuntus.org/)** - Designed for education and deployment in developing regions.
* **[Astra](https://astralinux.ru/en/)** / **[BOSS](https://www.bosslinux.in/)** / **[Pardus](https://www.pardus.org.tr/en/home/)** / **[UOS](https://www.chinauos.com/)** - State-sponsored distributions for Russia, India, Turkey, and China.
* **[openKylin](https://www.openkylin.top/downloads/os-en.html)** - The community-driven version of China's primary OS.
